1. Problem Statement
Walk through any Nigerian street, market, or neighbourhood fence and you will see it — handwritten on walls, gates, and shop fronts, scrawled in chalk or marker pen:

"SALESGIRL NEEDED. ENQUIRE WITHIN."       "EVENT WORKERS WANTED. CALL 080XXXXXXXX."       "LABOURERS NEEDED. NO EXPERIENCE."

This is how millions of Nigerian employers find workers today. A handwritten sign on a wall. A message broadcast to a WhatsApp group. A shout across a motor park. It is how it has always been done — and it is deeply broken.

On the worker's side, the picture is just as difficult. A university student who needs urgent cash for school fees cannot find a verified, safe weekend job at the click of a button. A young graduate waiting for formal employment has no platform to pick up hourly work to keep themselves afloat. A skilled artisan in Akure has no digital presence — their only marketing is word of mouth and hope.

Nigeria has an estimated 56 million informal workers and tens of millions of small employers. They exist in the same neighbourhoods, need each other, and yet have no trusted way to connect — safely, quickly, and fairly.

The Specific Pains This Creates
•	For employers: A shop owner writes on a wall and waits days for someone to walk in. An event planner sends WhatsApp blasts to groups and hopes enough people respond in time. A contractor stands at a motor park every morning selecting labourers by face with no knowledge of their reliability.
•	For workers: There is no searchable, verified pool of available jobs. A student who needs ₦5,000 by Friday has nowhere to look. A casual worker shows up for a job and is told to come back tomorrow — or simply never gets paid.
•	For both sides: There is no accountability. Workers disappear mid-job. Employers refuse to pay after work is done. There is no record, no recourse, and no trust infrastructure.
•	For safety: Workers — many of them young women — travel to unknown locations to meet strangers for work, with no verification that the employer is real, no record of where they went, and no mechanism to raise an alarm if something goes wrong.

The handwritten wall sign is not just an inconvenience. It is the symbol of an entire economy that has been left without the digital infrastructure it deserves.
 
2. Proposed Solution
WorkNow replaces the wall sign. It is a two-sided casual job marketplace that brings the informal Nigerian labour market onto a single, trusted, safe platform — without requiring anyone to be formal, have a CV, or speak perfect English.

A shop owner in Akure can post "Salesgirl needed — ₦35,000/month" in two minutes, and verified applicants in her area will see it immediately. An event planner in Lagos can post "Need 10 helpers for Saturday wedding" and have a full team confirmed by Thursday — with payment already secured in escrow. A university student who needs money for school fees can search for weekend jobs near campus, apply with one tap, work for a few hours, and withdraw their pay the same evening to their OPay wallet.

This is the core promise of WorkNow: the same job that was written on a wall is now posted on a platform — verified, safe, and paid.

For a Nigerian student, WorkNow is the difference between dropping out and staying in school. Flexible, safe, immediate income — from a phone, in minutes.

3. How It Works
For Workers (including students)
1.	Download WorkNow and register with a phone number — no CV, no formal credentials needed.
2.	Verify identity with NIN + facial scan to unlock all features. A tiered model means you can start browsing jobs immediately.
3.	Browse jobs nearby — filter by type (salary, hourly, one-off), pay rate, and distance.
4.	Apply with one tap. Get accepted. Log your resumption intent for safety.
5.	Clock in at the job site via GPS-verified check-in confirmed by the employer.
6.	Complete the job. Submit photo proof. Mark done.
7.	Get paid to your OPay wallet — daily for hourly jobs, instantly on approval for one-off jobs.
8.	Build a rating and badge history — making you more hireable and eventually eligible for micro-loans.

For Employers
9.	Register and verify identity with NIN + facial scan. No business registration required.
10.	Post a job in minutes — choose job type, set pay rate, duration, location, and number of workers needed.
11.	Upload a photo of the work environment — so workers know exactly what they are applying for.
12.	Deposit the full job amount into OPay escrow before the post goes live — your money is protected until work is verified complete.
13.	Review applicants by rating and badge tier. Accept the workers you trust.
14.	Confirm clock-in on resumption day. Approve work completion per worker.
15.	Escrow releases to workers. Platform fee deducted from your deposit — workers always receive their full agreed pay.
 
4. Addressing Unemployment and Underemployment
Nigeria's youth unemployment rate stands among the highest in Africa. But the problem is not simply a lack of jobs — it is a lack of accessible, trusted, and immediate connections to the work that already exists.

Jobs are being created every day in Nigeria's informal economy. Events happen every weekend. Shops need staff. Contractors need labour. Households need help. The demand for workers is real and constant. What is missing is the infrastructure to match that demand to the supply of willing workers quickly, safely, and fairly.

WorkNow directly attacks three dimensions of Nigeria's unemployment problem:

•	Frictional unemployment: People who want to work but cannot find available jobs fast enough. WorkNow eliminates this friction — jobs are searchable, nearby, and available in real time.
•	Underemployment: Skilled workers taking far below their capacity because no platform exists to showcase their track record. WorkNow's badge and rating system creates a verifiable work history that grows with every job.
•	Student and youth income gap: Young Nigerians in full-time education who need supplementary income but cannot take formal employment. WorkNow's per-hour and one-off models are designed precisely for this — flexible, immediate, no commitment beyond a single job.

Every job posted on WorkNow is a job that was previously advertised on a wall, shouted across a motor park, or never filled at all. WorkNow does not create artificial demand — it unlocks real demand that already exists.

5. OPay and Gemini Integration
OPay Everywhere — The Payment Backbone
OPay powers every financial transaction on WorkNow. This is not a cosmetic integration — OPay is the escrow layer, the wallet, and the withdrawal mechanism. Critically, OPay wallets require no traditional bank account — meaning the informal workers and students who need WorkNow most are not excluded by financial gatekeeping.
•	Employers deposit job funds directly from their OPay wallet — no bank transfer delays.
•	Escrow is held within OPay's licensed infrastructure — WorkNow never directly holds third-party funds.
•	Workers and students withdraw daily earnings instantly to their OPay wallet.
•	Monthly rewards, cancellation penalties, and dispute resolutions are all transacted via OPay.
•	Phase 2 micro-loans are disbursed and repaid through OPay — deducted automatically from future payouts.

OPay's existing user base in Nigeria's informal sector is WorkNow's cold-start advantage. WorkNow can be surfaced directly to OPay's millions of existing users as a featured service — many of whom are the exact workers and employers the platform is built for.

Google Gemini — The Intelligence Layer
Gemini is embedded across four functions in WorkNow, each solving a problem that rule-based systems cannot handle in the diversity and complexity of Nigeria's informal economy:
Function	What Gemini Does
Fraud detection	Scans every job post before it goes live — blocks suspicious pay rates, upfront fee requests, off-platform redirects, and scam language patterns.
Dispute triage	Reads both parties' reports in English or Pidgin, cross-references GPS logs and photo evidence. Low-literacy workers are protected — photo evidence weighs more than writing ability.
Review fraud monitoring	Flags fake 5-star review patterns — same parties rating each other repeatedly, instant reviews, or reviewers not physically present at the job location.
Behavioural monitoring	Tracks employer account patterns over time — repeated posting and cancellation, high dispute rates — and flags for admin review before workers are harmed.
 
6. Worker Safety — A Product Feature, Not a Policy
WorkNow is the first informal job platform in Nigeria to treat worker safety as a core product feature rather than a legal disclaimer. This is not accidental — it is the answer to one of the most serious barriers to informal work adoption, particularly among young women and students.

•	NIN + facial verification: Every user on the platform — employer and worker alike — is a verified real Nigerian adult. Workers know who they are going to work for before they leave home.
•	Resumption intent logging: Before a worker travels to a job, they log their expected arrival time on the app. If they do not clock in within one hour of that time, the platform alerts their emergency contact and flags the employer for review.
•	GPS tracking during shifts: Workers are located every 30 minutes during active shifts. If a worker arrives at a location significantly different from the posted job address, an alert fires immediately.
•	Emergency button: Visible and persistent on every active job screen — one tap shares the worker's GPS location with their emergency contact and creates a platform safety record.
•	7am–6pm hard restriction: No jobs, no clock-ins, no shift confirmations outside safe daylight hours. This is a system-level block, not a recommendation.
•	Post-job safety check-in: After every job, workers are asked: 'Did you feel safe today?' — with a one-tap report option if they did not.

A Nigerian mother should feel comfortable recommending WorkNow to her daughter. That is the safety standard we are building to.

9. Revenue Model
WorkNow charges employers — never workers. Workers always receive their full agreed pay. This is intentional: it builds the deep trust with the worker side that drives platform growth.

•	Transaction fee: Percentage of job value charged to employer at payment release.
•	Non-refundable listing fee: Small flat fee per job post to deter fake listings.
•	Cancellation penalty share: Platform retains a portion of late cancellation penalties.
•	Escrow float: Interest on funds held in escrow via OPay partnership.
•	Premium listings (Phase 3): Employers pay to boost posts to top of feed.
•	Micro-loan interest (Phase 4): Interest on loans to Gold-badge workers, repaid from future payouts.
